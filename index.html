About Rashtrapati Bhavan Museum

Rashtrapati Bhavan Museum is a world-class institution that offers a high-tech storytelling experience. Unlike traditional object-based museums, our museum focuses on event-based history, presenting the story of Rashtrapati Bhavan through cutting-edge technologies.

Experience the museum's unique narrative through virtual and augmented reality, interactive digital cascading tables, video walls, three-dimensional stereoscopic projections, holographic displays, and synchronized sound-light-video stage settings. Our digital storytelling approach ensures accessibility for all visitors, including those with speech and hearing impairments.

Spread across 11,000 square meters, the museum is ingeniously designed and built completely underground, preserving the heritage structures designed by architect Lutyens above it. Rashtrapati Bhavan Museum stands out as the latest technology-supported visitor-interactive storytelling museum in the country.

Major Attractions

President's Car

The President's Car at Rashtrapati Bhavan is a symbol of the country's dignity and grandeur. This vintage vehicle is an exquisite example of automotive luxury and craftsmanship, reflecting the opulence associated with the highest office in India. The car, a historic Rolls-Royce, has been used by the President for official functions, state visits, and ceremonial events, making it an iconic artifact of India's rich political heritage.

Drafting of Constitution

The drafting of the Indian Constitution was a monumental task, spearheaded by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar and the Constituent Assembly. This process involved detailed deliberations, debates, and negotiations to shape the foundational laws of independent India. The Museum showcases key documents, photographs, and artifacts from this historic period, offering visitors a glimpse into the creation of one of the world's longest-standing democratic frameworks.

Stone from the Moon

The Stone from the Moon is a rare and extraordinary artifact on display at our museum. This lunar rock sample was brought back to Earth by the Apollo missions and represents a significant achievement in space exploration. Visitors can marvel at its unique composition and learn about its journey from the lunar surface to our museum, offering a tangible connection to humanity's quest to explore beyond our planet.
